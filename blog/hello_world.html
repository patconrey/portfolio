Hello, world!

"Yay, another blog!" - Nobody ever.

September 24th, 2019.

My page already has an "About Me" section — a whole tab no less! So, why am I doing a blog as well? Does the internet need more clutter? Short answer, no. No, it doesn't.

I'm interested in the idea of learning publicly. I've always been one to hide away and study some topic, waiver on the fence about a question until I was certain of its answer. I'm not sure this approach has always been to my benefit. To be honest, I think there's a great deal of value to be wrought from learning in front of people, in trying to figure things out as you go, of being wrong and having somebody tell you why.

So, here I am, dedicating myself *publicly* to this aim! I will use this platform to learn, to explain my thoughts, to ask my questions. By volume, this blog will likely be tutorial-type posts about something I struggled with. Due to my digital signal processing course, I'll have ample inspiration.

I'm also excited by this because I've written the whole thing from scratch, which has been a blast so far. I'm thrilled to flesh out a blogging platform and will likely write a post or two about the things I learned and how I learned them. One of the big things I'm excited for is the integration with MathJax, a LaTeX rendering engine for the web! Check this out: \[ \delta (n - m) \Leftrightarrow z^{ - m} ,\left| z \right| > 1 \] For the interested reader, the above equation represents the Z-transform of a time-shifted \(\delta\) function. And it's directly embedded in the site! That's not a picture! I'm pumped!

Anyway, thanks for reading so far. I'm excited to see where this goes!
